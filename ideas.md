**Idea 1: Editorial Brutalism**
- **Design Movement**: Post-digital editorial brutalism meets luxury publishing
- **Core Principles**: Raw typographic hierarchy, deliberate asymmetry, confrontational whitespace, ink-on-paper materiality
- **Color Philosophy**: Warm ivory (#FCF9F4) as the canvas, deep charcoal (#1C1C19) for authority, amber-brown (#894D0D) as the provocateur — colors of aged paper, leather, and oxidized bronze
- **Layout Paradigm**: Offset grid with oversized type bleeding off-screen, full-bleed image panels alternating with dense text columns, no centered layouts
- **Signature Elements**: Oversized italic numerals as section dividers, thin ruled lines as structural scaffolding, grayscale images that reveal color on hover
- **Interaction Philosophy**: Hover reveals color in a grayscale world — metaphor for awakening potential
- **Animation**: Slow, deliberate fade-ins on scroll, text that appears letter by letter for key headlines, image desaturation that lifts on hover
- **Typography System**: Newsreader (serif, italic for display) + Inter (sans-serif, tight tracking for labels) — the tension between classical authority and modern precision

**Idea 2: Minimalist Luxury**
- **Design Movement**: Swiss International Style meets contemporary luxury brand identity
- **Core Principles**: Extreme restraint, mathematical spacing, typographic supremacy, purposeful negative space
- **Color Philosophy**: Near-white backgrounds with single accent — the luxury of saying less
- **Layout Paradigm**: Strict vertical rhythm, left-aligned text blocks, full-width image moments
- **Signature Elements**: Thin horizontal rules, micro-typography labels, oversized pull quotes
- **Interaction Philosophy**: Invisible until needed — interactions feel discovered, not announced
- **Animation**: Opacity transitions only, no movement
- **Typography System**: Single typeface family with extreme weight contrast

## Selected: Idea 1 — Editorial Brutalism

This approach best honors the Google Stitch design intent: warm ivory backgrounds, amber-brown primary, grayscale-to-color image reveals, oversized italic type, and the confrontational editorial voice of the Wendelin Monplaisir brand.
